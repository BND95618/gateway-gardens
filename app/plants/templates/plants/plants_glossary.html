A specific plant variety that has been intentionally bred by humans to possess desirable characteristics, meaning it is cultivated and maintained through human intervention; essentially, a cultivar is a "cultivated variety" of a plant species. Fertilizer NPK

The NPK ratio represents the availability of Nitrogen, Phosphorous, and Potassium, by weight, contained in a plant fertilizer.

  • N = Nitrogen: Helps leaves grow and gives plants a deep green color
  • P = Phosphorus: Helps roots develop and absorb nutrients
  • K = Potasium: Helps plants convert food into energy and strengthens plant blades

Hybrid

A hybrid plant is the offspring of a cross between two different species or varieties. Hybrids have greater genetic variability than their parents. Hybrid plants tend to be more vigorous than their parents, and often have faster growth, increased yield, and greater size. Hybrids cannot usually be reproduced by seed and must be clonally reproduced (i.e cuttings, grafting, or tissue culture).

Species

A natural grouping of similar plants (or organisms) that can interbreed. Usually, a species develops over time when the plant group is isolated and through inbreeding becomes a unique species.

Naming: Lower case and italize.

Sunset Zones

Sunset climate zones consider the total climate. Including the following factors:

  • Winter minimum temperature
  • Summer maximum temperature
  • Length of growing season
  • Humidity
  • Rainfall patterns (timing and amount)
  • Wind

Variety

In plant taxonomy, a variety is a plant within a species group in nature that forms a trait. The trait is stable and can be reproduced either by seed or by cuttings or grafting. A naturally occurring variation within a plant species, often arising from genetic mutations or environmental factors.
